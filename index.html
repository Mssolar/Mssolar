Solar PV Knowledge

Understand the basics of how solar photovoltaic (PV) systems work to generate clean energy for your home or business.

A solar PV system is composed of solar panels (modules), a mounting system, and an inverter with a computerized controller. Solar panels are made of silicon, which acts as a semiconductor. When sunlight (photons) hits the silicon, it excites electrons, creating a direct current (DC) of electricity. This is known as the photovoltaic effect.

The DC electricity then flows from the panels to an inverter. The inverter's crucial job is to convert the DC electricity into alternating current (AC) electricity. AC is the standard form of electricity used to power appliances in our homes and businesses. This usable AC power then flows from the inverter to your electrical panel (breaker box) and is distributed throughout your property.
